3. **When several pushes or pulls act on one object at once, they combine by direction:** pushes in the same direction add their paces; pushes in opposing directions subtract. The object moves at the combined pace in the direction of the larger side, or stays put if the two sides match.

4. **Falling is a steady downward pull by the world itself, of a strength matched to the object's heaviness** — so the resulting downward pace is the same for every object, light or heavy, and does not grow as the fall continues.
