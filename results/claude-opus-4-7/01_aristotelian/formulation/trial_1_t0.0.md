**1. Natural place and natural motion.**
- *Operational form:* Every body has a kind. Bodies of earthy kind (iron, stone, pebble, water) tend toward the earth; bodies of airy/fiery kind (smoke, flame) tend toward the sky. When no push, pull, or thicker surrounding holds it in place, a body moves along the straight line joining it to its natural place. **2. Weight as strength of downward tendency.**

- *Scope:* Earthy bodies at rest on, or being dragged along, a yielding surface; or being held against their downward tendency. Comparative only — gives an ordering, not a numerical law.

- *Scope:* Terrestrial bodies undergoing forced (non-natural) motion.

**7. Lingering push.**
