# Operational Rules

1. **Effort is required for motion.**
   - *Operational form:* If at a given instant no push or pull acts on a body, its pace at that instant is zero. Equivalently: pace > 0 only while a push or pull is being applied.
   - *Scope:* All bodies, in all surroundings (surface, open air, evacuated jar).
   - *Changes / stays the same:* The body's location changes while a push acts; the moment the push ceases, the location stays the same thereafter (until another push acts). No quantity of motion is preserved across the moment of release.
   - *Boundary cases:* A body in free fall is always under the downward pull (rule 7), so this rule never leaves a falling body "unpushed" in midair.

2. **Pace is established immediately, with no run-up.**
   - *Operational form:* At the instant a push of strength E begins to act on a body of heaviness H, the pace is already E/H. There is no interval during which the pace is smaller than E/H.
   - *Scope:* Any application, removal, or change of push. Applies symmetrically to starting, stopping, and changing the push.
   - *Changes / stays the same:* Pace changes discontinuously with effort; between changes of effort, pace stays constant.
   - *Boundary cases:* The rule treats the transition as instantaneous; observations do not test what happens during the transition itself.

3. **Pace is proportional to push.**
   - *Operational form:* For a fixed body (fixed H), if push E₁ produces pace P₁ and push E₂ produces pace P₂, then P₁/P₂ = E₁/E₂. In particular, E = 0 gives P = 0.
   - *Scope:* Steady pushes along a single line of action, on a single body.
   - *Changes / stays the same:* The ratio pace/push stays the same for a given body across different pushes; the value of that ratio is 1/H.
   - *Boundary cases:* None among the listed observations.

4. **Pace is inversely proportional to heaviness.**
   - *Operational form:* Combining with rule 3: **P = E / H**, where P is the pace produced, E is the strength of the push, and H is the heaviness of the body. Given any two of {P, E, H}, the third is fixed.
   - *Scope:* Pushed or pulled motion of a single body by a single net effort (or by a combined effort computed via rule 6).
   - *Changes / stays the same:* H is a property of the body and stays the same throughout the motions described. P changes only when E changes.
   - *Boundary cases:* The rule presumes H > 0; the observations include no massless bodies.

5. **Surroundings are irrelevant to the pace law.**
   - *Operational form:* The value of P = E/H does not depend on whether the body is on a track, in open air, in a jar emptied of air, or touching nothing. Substituting one surrounding for another, with the same E and H, gives the same P.
   - *Scope:* All settings represented in the observations.
   - *Changes / stays the same:* The surroundings may change; P, given E and H, does not.
   - *Boundary cases:* The observations do not include surroundings that themselves exert a push (e.g., a strong wind, a current); such a push would have to be counted into E by rule 6.

6. **Combination of pushes by signed addition along a line.**
   - *Operational form:* If pushes E₁, E₂, … act along one line, assign each a sign according to direction (+ one way, − the other). The net effort is E = E₁ + E₂ + …. The resulting pace is P = E / H, directed the way of the sign of E. If E = 0, the body is at rest.
   - *Scope:* Pushes on a single body acting along a common line. The observations do not cover pushes at angles.
   - *Changes / stays the same:* The arithmetic sum of signed efforts at any instant determines the pace at that instant; nothing accumulates across instants.
   - *Boundary cases:* Non-collinear pushes are outside the scope of the observations.

7. **The falling pull and the common falling pace.**
   - *Operational form:* On every body there acts a downward pull of strength E_fall = g·H, where g is a fixed strength-per-heaviness characteristic of this world. By rule 4, the resulting downward pace is P_fall = E_fall / H = g, the same for every body. P_fall is constant from the instant of release onward; it does not grow with the duration of the fall.
   - *Scope:* Any body not supported and not held; applies whether or not other pushes also act (those combine by rule 6).
   - *Changes / stays the same:* The downward pace stays the same at value g throughout the fall. The body's height changes; its falling pace does not.
   - *Boundary cases:* The numerical value of g is not fixed by the observations; it is a given feature of this world. The rules do not say what happens at the moment of impact.

8. **Carriage lends no motion.**
   - *Operational form:* While a body is held by a carrier moving at pace P_c, the body's pace equals P_c only because the carrier's grip supplies the push that produces it (by rule 4). At the instant the grip is released, that push vanishes; the only remaining push is the downward fall pull (rule 7). Therefore from the instant of release the body moves straight downward at pace g, regardless of the carrier's prior pace.
   - *Scope:* Any body released from a carrier (hand, vehicle, etc.) in any surroundings.
   - *Changes / stays the same:* The horizontal component of pace changes discontinuously from P_c to 0 at release; the vertical component changes discontinuously from 0 to g at release. Nothing of the carrier's motion is retained by the released body.
   - *Boundary cases:* This is the rule most sharply distinguished from a world in which carried motion is retained; the observations affirm the straight-down drop but do not record fine timing of the transition.

9. **Transport requires continuous pushing.**
   - *Operational form:* To move a body of heaviness H from point A to point B at pace P along the way, a push of strength E = P·H must act continuously over the whole path. If the push ends at any intermediate point, the body's pace becomes 0 there (rule 1) and it remains at that point.
   - *Scope:* Horizontal or otherwise non-falling transport. For motion against the fall pull, the push must additionally overcome g·H by rule 6.
   - *Changes / stays the same:* The body's position changes; the required push stays the same for steady pace; if the push is removed, position stays the same thereafter.
   - *Boundary cases:* The observations do not include cases where the load's heaviness changes mid-transport.

# Boundary notes

The operational rules leave the following observations only partly covered:
- **Observation 10 (two objects from the same height strike the ground together):** the rules predict same-pace-from-the-first-instant, which entails same arrival time, but the observations record only the arrival, not the pace history. The stronger reading is assumed.
- **Observation 11 (the value of the falling pace):** the rules fix that the falling pace is the same for all bodies and constant in time, but they do not determine its numerical value g; this is taken as a given feature of the world.
- **The instant of release (rule 8) and the instant of impact (rule 7):** the rules describe the pace immediately before and immediately after these instants but do not describe the transition itself; the observations do not resolve it either.
- **Non-collinear pushes** and **surroundings that themselves push** (winds, currents) are not represented in the observations and are outside the operational scope of rule 6.
