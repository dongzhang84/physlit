# Operational Rules

1. **Pace follows push, not change of push.**
   - *Operational form:* At every instant, a body's pace is determined entirely by the push acting on it at that same instant. The pace is a single-valued function of the present push: same push now, same pace now, regardless of what the push was a moment before.
   - *Scope:* All bodies, whether being pushed along a track, carried, or falling.
   - *What changes / stays the same:* The pace changes whenever and only when the push changes. Nothing about the body's prior motion is retained.
   - *Boundary:* If the push changes abruptly, the pace changes abruptly with it (see rule 3).

2. **No carry-over of motion.**
   - *Operational form:* If, at any instant, the total push on a body is zero, then the body's pace at that instant is zero. There is no quantity stored in the body from earlier pushes.
   - *Scope:* All bodies in all circumstances.
   - *What changes / stays the same:* Nothing quantitative is preserved across time. The only thing that "stays the same" is the rule itself: zero push always means zero pace.
   - *Boundary:* This rule, together with rule 8, requires that a falling body's "zero push" condition never occurs while it falls — the downward pull is always present.

3. **Pace begins and ends at once.**
   - *Operational form:* When a push of given strength is applied to a body starting at time T, the body's pace takes its full value at T and at every instant thereafter while the push lasts. When the push ends at time T′, the pace is zero at T′ and after. There is no interval of speeding up or slowing down.
   - *Scope:* All pushed motion.
   - *What changes / stays the same:* Pace is a step-function of time, mirroring the push. Within an interval of steady push, the pace stays the same.
   - *Boundary:* Requires that pushes themselves be treated as either present or absent at each instant; gradual changes in push produce gradual changes in pace, in step.

4 & 5 (combined into the central pace law).

4. **Pace grows in step with effort.**
   - *Operational form:* For a body of fixed heaviness, pace is directly proportional to the push: if push P gives pace V, then push kP gives pace kV, for any positive number k.
   - *Scope:* A single body under a single push along one line.
   - *What changes / stays the same:* The ratio (pace ÷ push) stays the same for a given body; the pace itself varies with the push.
   - *Boundary:* Says nothing on its own about comparing different bodies — that requires rule 5.

5. **Heaviness resists pace.**
   - *Operational form:* For a fixed push, pace is inversely proportional to heaviness: a body of heaviness H has half the pace of a body of heaviness H/2 under the same push. Combining with rule 4:
     **pace = push ÷ heaviness.**
     Given any push P and any heaviness H, the resulting pace is P/H.
   - *Scope:* Any body under any single push, excluding the downward pull (which is treated by rule 8).
   - *What changes / stays the same:* For a given body, heaviness stays the same; pace tracks push. Across bodies under equal push, the product (pace × heaviness) stays the same.
   - *Boundary:* Applied naively to falling, this would predict heavier things fall slower; rule 8 supplies the special stipulation that resolves this.

6. **The track plays no part.**
   - *Operational form:* The value P/H for pace under push P on a body of heaviness H is the same whether the body is on a track, on open ground, or in open space touching nothing. No additional push from or against the surface enters the calculation.
   - *Scope:* Any sustained pushed motion, on any supporting surface or none.
   - *What changes / stays the same:* The pace law is invariant under change of surroundings (other than air, which is addressed in rule 9).
   - *Boundary:* Does not address what happens if the surface itself moves or tilts; the rules so far assume level support or none.

7. **Pushes in the same line add and subtract.**
   - *Operational form:* If pushes P₁, P₂, … act on a body of heaviness H along one line, with signs according to direction, the pace is (P₁ + P₂ + …)/H, in the direction of the resulting sign. If the sum is zero, the pace is zero.
   - *Scope:* Multiple pushes along a single line on one body.
   - *What changes / stays the same:* The pace is set by the running total of pushes at each instant; nothing across instants is preserved.
   - *Boundary:* The rules as stated cover only collinear pushes; pushes at an angle are not addressed.

8. **Falling is a steady downward pull matched to heaviness.**
   - *Operational form:* Every body, while falling, experiences a downward pull whose strength equals a fixed quantity G times the body's heaviness: pull = G·H. By rule 5, the resulting downward pace is (G·H)/H = G, the same single value for every body. This pace is constant throughout the fall — it does not grow with time or distance.
   - *Scope:* Any body falling freely, from the instant the supporting or carrying push is removed.
   - *What changes / stays the same:* The downward pace stays the same throughout the fall and is the same across all bodies. What changes is only the body's position.
   - *Boundary:* This rule is a stipulation about the downward pull (it must scale exactly with heaviness); it is not derived from the other rules.

9. **The downward pull does not depend on air.**
   - *Operational form:* The quantity G in rule 8 is the same in open air and in a jar emptied of air. The presence or absence of air does not enter the pace calculation for falling.
   - *Scope:* Falling bodies in any air condition, including none.
   - *What changes / stays the same:* G stays the same across air conditions; the falling pace is unchanged.
   - *Boundary:* Says nothing about whether air itself can supply additional sideways pushes; the rules so far do not treat air as a pusher.

10. **A thing carried shares only the carrier's present push.**
    - *Operational form:* While a carrier holds a body and moves it with pace V_c, the body's pace equals V_c. At the instant of release, the carrier's push on the body becomes zero; from that instant only the downward pull (rule 8) acts, so the body's pace is zero in the carrier's former direction and G downward. The body therefore descends along the vertical line through the point of release.
    - *Scope:* Any body released from a carrier moving in any direction.
    - *What changes / stays the same:* Before release, pace matches the carrier's; at release, the forward pace drops to zero at once (by rule 3) and the downward pace becomes G at once (by rule 8).
    - *Boundary:* This is the sharpest consequence of rules 2 and 3; if observation shows a released body continuing forward, this rule fails.

11. **To move a load, keep pushing.**
    - *Operational form:* A body reaches a destination at distance D from its starting place only if a push is applied to it continuously over the entire interval during which it covers that distance. The time required, under steady push P on heaviness H, is D ÷ (P/H) = D·H/P. The moment pushing stops, the body's pace becomes zero and it advances no further.
    - *Scope:* All transport of loads along a line.
    - *What changes / stays the same:* Distance covered changes in proportion to elapsed pushing time; pace stays the same while push stays the same.
    - *Boundary:* Combined with rule 10, this means a thrown body cannot reach a horizontally distant target — it falls straight down at release.
