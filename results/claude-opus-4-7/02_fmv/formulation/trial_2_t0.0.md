# Operational Rules

1. **Effort and pace are simultaneous.**
   - *Operational form:* At every instant t, if a push of strength P acts on a body, the body has pace V at that same instant t. If P = 0 at instant t, then V = 0 at instant t. There is no time delay between change in push and change in pace.
   - *Scope:* All bodies, all situations involving pushes or pulls applied by a carrier, hand, or other external source.
   - *What changes / what stays the same:* The pace changes instantly whenever the push changes. Nothing about the past push is retained by the body — no quantity carries forward.
   - *Boundary cases:* The rule treats the transition (push on → push off) as instantaneous; the observations do not show what happens during the transition itself.

2. **Pace is steady under steady push.**
   - *Operational form:* If push P is held constant from time t₁ to t₂, then pace V is constant and equal to its full value throughout [t₁, t₂]. V does not depend on how long P has been applied.
   - *Scope:* Any body under a single sustained push.
   - *What changes / what stays the same:* Under steady push, the pace stays the same; the position changes uniformly. Duration of push does not enter as a quantity affecting pace.
   - *Boundary cases:* None among the observations.

3 & 4. **Pace = effort divided by heaviness.**
   - *Operational form:* For a body of heaviness H subjected to a net push of strength P in a given direction, the pace is V = P / H, in the direction of P. Doubling P doubles V; doubling H halves V.
   - *Scope:* Any body acted on by a single push, or by a net push obtained from rule 5. Applies to pushed motion along a track and to bodies adrift (rule 6).
   - *What changes / what stays the same:* While P and H are fixed, V is fixed. If P changes, V changes in the same proportion at the same instant. H is taken as a fixed property of the body across the observations.
   - *Boundary cases:* Rule 4 must be applied with care to falling (see rule 9): the downward pull is not an externally chosen P but is itself proportional to H.

5. **Combination of pushes by direction.**
   - *Operational form:* If pushes P₁, P₂, … act on the same body along one line, with P_i positive in one chosen direction and negative in the opposite, then the net push is P = Σ P_i, and the body's pace is V = P / H in the sign of P. If P = 0, then V = 0.
   - *Scope:* Any body under simultaneous multiple pushes along a single line. The observations do not fix how pushes at oblique angles combine.

   - *Boundary cases:* None.

   - *Boundary cases:* None beyond those in rules 1–2.
