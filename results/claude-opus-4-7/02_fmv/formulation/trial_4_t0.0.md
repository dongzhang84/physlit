# Operational Rules

**1. A thing moves only while it is being pushed or pulled.**
- *Operational form:* At any instant t, if no push or pull acts on a body, its pace at t is zero (it occupies the same place at t+Δt as at t). If a push begins at instant t₀ and ends at instant t₁, the body has nonzero pace only during the open interval (t₀, t₁), and is at rest at all instants outside it.
- *Scope:* All bodies, whether resting on a track or adrift, in air or in a jar emptied of air.
- *What changes / stays the same:* The body's location changes only while a push is applied. Whether the body is in a state of "being pushed" or "being at rest" is the variable; the rule itself (the if–then linkage) stays the same.
- *Boundary cases:* A held but stationary object (e.g., a stone in a still hand) has no pace; consistent. A held and moving object (a stone in a swinging hand) is being pushed sideways by the hand; consistent.

**2. The pace tracks the push instant by instant.**
- *Operational form:* At each instant t, pace(t) is determined entirely by the push P(t) and heaviness H acting at that same instant t. There is no dependence on P at any earlier or later instant. If P jumps from 0 to P₀ at t₀, pace jumps from 0 to its full value at t₀ with no transition interval.
- *Scope:* All bodies under any push, including the downward pull.
- *What changes / stays the same:* Pace changes in lockstep with push. Heaviness H of a given body stays the same throughout.
- *Boundary cases:* None among the observations; the instantaneous-response clause is a stipulation, not derived.

**3 & 4 (combined). Pace = effort ÷ heaviness, along the direction of the push.**
- *Operational form:* For a body of heaviness H under a single push of effort E in direction d, the pace is E/H in direction d. Doubling E doubles the pace; doubling H halves it. The pace has the same direction as the push.
- *Scope:* Any body under a single push (or a single net push, after Rule 5 is applied). Applies on tracks and in open space alike.
- *What changes / stays the same:* Pace changes if either E or the direction of the push changes. H is a fixed property of the body. The ratio E/H is the pace at that instant.
- *Boundary cases:* The rule requires a unit-bearing convention: "effort 1 on heaviness 1 produces pace 1." Without a reference standard the proportionality is only comparative.

**5. Combining pushes.**
- *Operational form:* Given pushes E₁ in direction d and E₂ in direction d (same direction), treat as a single push of effort E₁+E₂ in direction d. Given E₁ in direction d and E₂ in the opposite direction, treat as a single push of effort |E₁−E₂| in the direction of the larger, or zero net push if E₁=E₂. Then apply Rule 3&4 to this combined push.
- *Scope:* Any body with two or more pushes acting at the same instant, including the downward pull when it is in effect.
- *What changes / stays the same:* The net push (and so the pace) can change instant by instant as the individual pushes change. Heaviness is unchanged.

- *Scope:* All bodies, on any surface or none.

- *Boundary cases:* None.

- *Scope:* Released bodies (see Rule 8). Operates in air or in a jar emptied of air.
- *What changes / stays the same:* The downward pace k is the same number for every released body and at every instant of release. H differs between bodies but cancels.
- *Boundary cases:* No observation tells us whether k changes with location (e.g., higher up). Treated as constant.

## Boundary notes

The operational rules above do not cover the following:

- **Non-collinear combinations of pushes.** Rule 5 specifies only same-direction addition and opposite-direction subtraction. Two pushes at an angle have no specified resultant, because no observation involved such a case.
- **The moment of release itself.** Rules 1–2 demand that pace responds instantly to push; Rule 8 demands that the downward pull switches on instantly at release. The instant of release is therefore a discontinuity in both the sideways pace (which drops to zero) and the downward pace (which jumps to k). The rules describe the before and after states but do not describe what happens *at* the instant t of release; this is left as a sharp transition.
- **The held-and-moving stone (Observation 11).** The Tension paragraph already flagged this. Rule 8 covers it only by stipulation: while held, the downward pull is set aside; on release, the sideways push from the hand ceases (by Rule 1) and the downward pull resumes (by Rule 8). The rules predict a straight-down drop from the point of release, matching the observation, but only because Rule 8 was added for that purpose.
- **Reference units.** Rule 3&4 is operational only once a reference pairing of effort and heaviness is fixed. The observations do not supply one explicitly.
- **Spatial variation of k.** Rule 7 treats the downward-pull constant k as the same everywhere; no observation tests this.
