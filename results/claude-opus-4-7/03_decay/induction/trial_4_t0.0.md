**Rule 3 — The fade is per unit elapsed time, not per swing or per circuit.**
Because the fraction lost depends on seconds elapsed, a slow pendulum (long swing time) loses a larger fraction of its amplitude per completed swing than a fast one — its swing simply takes more seconds, during which more fading has occurred. **Rule 4 — A steady downward pull acts on every weight, with the same pull-per-weight for all materials.**
This pull is what makes pendulums swing and balls fall.
