# Operational rules

**Rule 1 — Multiplicative fade of motion.**

1. *Operational form.* Let A(t) denote the current value of a motion-amplitude: the peak displacement of an oscillation, the speed of a moving body along its path, or the spin rate of a turning body. Then for any elapsed time Δt (in seconds) during which the system is left to itself,
  A(t + Δt) = A(t) · r^Δt,
 with r = 0.990 per second (equivalently, A falls to 1/e of its starting value in about 100 s). Given A(0) and Δt, a third party computes A(Δt) = A(0) · (0.990)^Δt.

2. *Scope.* Applies to any system whose state can be summarised by one of these scalar amplitudes: the peak swing of a pendulum, the peak stretch of a spring oscillation, the speed of a sphere along a track, the sideways speed of an orbiting body, the spin rate of a top, the peak vibration of a struck bell's rim. "Isolated" means: not being struck, pushed, or pulled along its direction of motion by an outside agent during the interval Δt. The rule is indifferent to the weight, material, or surroundings of the moving body (Obs. 10).

3. *What changes / what stays the same.* The amplitude A shrinks monotonically toward zero. The per-second retention ratio r stays the same — it is the one quantity that is steady across systems and across time. No amplitude-like quantity of the isolated system holds steady; the only steady thing is the rate at which amplitudes shrink.

4. *Boundary cases.* The rule as stated would also drive a falling body's downward speed to zero, which contradicts Obs. 3. Rule 3 patches this for the downward direction.

**Rule 2 — The fade rate is a property of elapsed seconds, not of the moving body.**

1. *Operational form.* The factor r = 0.990 per second in Rule 1 does not depend on the body's weight, material, size, or on the period of the motion. Per cycle of a periodic motion of period T seconds, the per-cycle retention is r^T = (0.990)^T. So a pendulum of period 2 s retains (0.990)^2 ≈ 0.980 of its swing per swing, while one of period 4 s retains (0.990)^4 ≈ 0.961 per swing.

2. *Scope.* Applies wherever Rule 1 applies. Lets a third party predict, from the period alone, the per-cycle shrinkage of any oscillating or rotating system.

 Horizontal speed v_h, meanwhile, follows Rule 1 unmodified: v_h(t + Δt) = v_h(t) · r^Δt. The two directions evolve independently. A third party predicting a cannon shot (Obs. 7) computes v_h and v_d separately and adds the resulting displacements.
