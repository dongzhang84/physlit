**2. Imparted Drive and Effort**
*   **Operational form:** An object moves horizontally if and only if it possesses an "imparted drive." This drive requires an applied "effort." If effort is applied continuously (by a pusher), horizontal motion continues indefinitely. If effort is applied impulsively (by a launcher), the object receives a finite imparted drive and continues forward free of the launcher. For any given setup, the effort required to produce motion is strictly ranked by the amount of material: a heavier load requires a visibly greater effort to move than a lighter load.

*   **Boundary cases:** The rule provides a comparative ranking (more material = more effort) but does not provide an exact mathematical proportionality to predict the exact effort required for a completely new, unmeasured heavy load.

**3. Resistance of Surroundings**
*   **Operational form:** Any physical surrounding continuously subtracts from an object's imparted horizontal drive and applies an upward opposition against natural downward motion.
    *   *Thickness ranking:* Resistance strictly increases with the thickness of the surroundings: thin air < pond water < thick honey < dirt road.
