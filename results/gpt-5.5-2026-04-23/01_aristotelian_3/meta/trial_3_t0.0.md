**Q4. Without naming the framework, what are the major differences from standard physics?**

The framework I used differs from standard physics in several consequential ways.

1. **Motion is explained by natural places and kinds, not by forces and acceleration.**

In the used framework, bodies move because of what kind of body they are:

> “Earthy or watery bodies: natural place is below”

> “Fiery or smoky bodies: natural place is above”

In standard physics, falling, rising smoke, and floating are not explained by intrinsic natural places. They are explained through gravity, pressure gradients, buoyancy, fluid dynamics, density, and forces.

2. **Continued horizontal motion is treated as needing continued urging, except for a temporary projectile carry-over.**

The used framework says:

> “For motion that is not the body’s natural motion, continued urging is required”

and for arrows adds:

> “temporary carried motion… fades”

Standard physics instead treats uniform motion as persisting unless acted on by a net external force. A cart stops because of friction and drag, not because motion itself requires continued pushing. A projectile continues horizontally because of inertia, not because of a temporary carried motion that is gradually used up.

3. **Projectiles are handled by a special fading-motion account rather than inertia plus gravity.**

The used framework says:

> “the sending action leaves a temporary carried motion in the body or in the air about it”

and:

> “as the carried motion fades, the body’s natural motion reasserts itself.”

Standard physics would say the arrow has horizontal velocity after launch; gravity accelerates it downward; air resistance modifies both components. There is no need for a special “carried motion” that is deposited in the arrow or surrounding air.

4. **Falling speed is treated qualitatively by kind, medium, and ease of delay, not by a quantitative dynamics of mass, gravity, drag, and buoyancy.**

The used framework says:

> “A thicker surrounding stuff delays motion more than a thinner one”

and:

> “a pea falls more slowly than an iron ball because… it is more easily delayed by the air.”

Standard physics distinguishes gravitational force, inertial mass, drag force, terminal velocity, cross-sectional area, density, and fluid viscosity. In vacuum, bodies fall with the same gravitational acceleration regardless of mass, ignoring relativistic/tidal complications. The used framework had no general vacuum rule and no equations.

5. **Celestial motion is placed in a separate category.**

The used framework says:

> “Heavenly bodies… naturally travel in daily circles”

Standard physics does not assign heavenly bodies a fundamentally separate kind of natural circular motion. Their apparent daily paths are mainly due to Earth’s rotation, and their actual motions are described using the same mechanics and gravity that apply terrestrially, with appropriate scales and reference frames.
