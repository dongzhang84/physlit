**Q4. How does this framework differ from standard physics?**

Without naming the historical framework, the biggest differences are these:

### 1. Motion is explained by natural tendencies toward places, not by universal forces and acceleration laws.

The framework says things like:

> “earthy and stony bodies… naturally seek the lower region”

and:

> “fiery, smoky, and flame-like things naturally seek the upper region.”

Standard physics would not classify matter by intrinsic desires for upper or lower places. It would use gravity, buoyancy, pressure gradients, density, drag, and thermodynamics.

### 2. Continued horizontal motion requires explanation by a mover or a temporary carried-over tendency.

The framework says:

> “A body being pushed or pulled continues its travel only while the mover keeps acting on it, unless some carried-over motion remains for a time.”

and:

> “This forward tendency gradually fades as the arrow passes through air.”

Standard physics instead treats uniform motion as persisting unless acted on by a net external force. It does not require a continuing mover to sustain motion. The default is inertia, not rest.

### 3. Heavier bodies are expected to fall faster, unless the medium interferes.

The framework states:

> “Heavier downward-seeking bodies descend more strongly than lighter ones when the air does not greatly hinder them.”

Standard physics says that, neglecting air resistance, bodies near Earth fall with the same gravitational acceleration regardless of mass. Differences in falling speed in air are due primarily to drag, shape, density, and terminal velocity, not because heavier bodies have a stronger natural downward tendency in the same sense.

### 4. Projectile motion is not analyzed as independent horizontal and vertical components governed by inertia and gravity.

The framework says:

> “early flight → chiefly forward; later flight → increasingly downward; final motion → descent to earth.”

and explains this by:

> “carried-over forward tendency fades.”

Standard physics would say the arrow’s horizontal velocity persists except for drag, while gravity accelerates it downward from the beginning. The downward motion does not wait for the forward tendency to fade, even though the trajectory curves gradually.

### 5. Celestial bodies are assigned a separate kind of motion.

The framework says:

> “The heavenly lights belong to a separate upper order whose natural motion is circular.”

Standard physics applies the same underlying mechanics to celestial and terrestrial bodies. It does not treat stars, planets, stones, arrows, and carts as belonging to fundamentally different mechanical orders.
