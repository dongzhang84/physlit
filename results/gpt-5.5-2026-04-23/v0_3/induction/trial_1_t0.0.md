   - This also explains why a candle flame points upward even when the candle itself is tilted sideways.

2. **A surrounding substance can hinder or carry a moving thing, and different surrounding substances hinder to different degrees.**
   - Air hinders falling or flying things somewhat.
   - Honey hinders much more strongly than air.
   - Moving air can carry light loose things sideways.
   - Therefore a pebble settles slowly in honey but falls quickly through air; feather-down drifts and may be carried aside by a slight breeze; arrows do not continue straight forever through air.

3. **The amount of hindrance depends on a thing’s shape, spread, and heaviness.**
   - Broad, thin, or loose things are hindered more by air than compact things.
   - Heavier compact things overcome the hindrance of air better than lighter or more spread-out things.
   - Therefore an iron ball falls faster than a dried pea; a compact metal ball falls faster than the same weight of metal hammered into a thin sheet; a feather-down settles much more gently than an iron bar.

4. **A thing placed in water either sinks or remains at the surface according to how well its kind and form agree with water’s support.**
   - Heavy stony and metallic things do not stay supported by water and sink.
   - Dry oak is supported by water and remains at the surface.
   - Thus a heavy stone sinks straight to the pond bottom, while a piece of dry oak floats.

5. **A thing being pushed, pulled, or shot can be made to move in a direction different from its natural tendency, but that imparted motion fades after the mover is no longer acting on it.**

   - An arrow released by a bow continues forward for many paces because the bow has given it forward motion, but that motion gradually fades, while the arrow’s downward tendency also shows itself, so its path curves downward and it strikes the earth.

6. **The more heavily burdened a thing is, the harder it is for a living puller or pusher to keep it moving along the ground.**

7. **The Sun, Moon, and fixed stars belong to the sky and naturally move in daily circles around the world.**
