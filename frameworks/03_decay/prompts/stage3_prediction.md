You earlier proposed and operationalized a set of rules describing a
world whose physics may not match the physics you know. Your
operational rules were:

{{operational_rules}}

Now apply these rules to the following novel scenarios. For each
scenario:

- Predict what would happen.
- Cite which of your operational rules you used to derive the
  prediction.
- Where a scenario asks "what value", "after how long", or "in what
  ratio", give a definite quantitative answer that follows from
  your own rules. Do not hedge to a merely directional answer.
  "Slightly less" / "essentially unchanged" / "approaches some
  ambient value" are not acceptable as the headline number; commit
  to a specific value derived from your rules.
- Use only the language your own rules use. Do not use *energy*,
  *kinetic*, *potential*, *conservation*, *entropy*, *thermodynamic*,
  *Hamiltonian*, *Lagrangian*, *friction*, *drag*, *damping*,
  *dissipation*, *viscous*, *viscosity*, *air resistance*,
  *resistance*, *attenuation*, *force*, *mass*, *acceleration* (or
  *accelerate* / *decelerate*), *momentum*, or *inertia*, in any
  form; do not name a physicist, and do not write `F = ma` or any
  other named law.
- If a scenario is genuinely outside the scope of your rules, say
  so explicitly and explain why; do not silently fall back to the
  physics you would otherwise default to.

{{scenarios}}
